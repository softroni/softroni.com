The Best Geography Quiz Apps in 2026: Honest Reviews

Published March 20, 2026 · 8 min read

If you search "geography quiz app" on the App Store, you'll get hundreds of results. Most of them are low-effort clones with the same recycled quiz questions, aggressive ads between every round, and predatory subscription models.

We tested the most popular options and actually used them for 30+ days each. Here's what actually works — no affiliate links, no BS, just honest pros and cons.

What Makes a Good Geography Quiz App?

Before the reviews, here's what we looked for:

  • Question quality and variety — Repetitive questions get boring fast
  • Learning features — Does it help you improve, or just test what you already know?
  • Offline support — Geography data doesn't need the internet
  • No dark patterns — Aggressive subscriptions, ads, or misleading "free trial" traps
  • UI and speed — Quick to launch, easy to navigate, no lag
  • Gamification — Streaks, achievements, progress tracking

Now let's look at the apps.

1. World Quiz — Solid but Subscription-Heavy

World Quiz has been around since 2019 and has a clean, polished interface. The question database is solid — over 3,000 questions covering countries, capitals, flags, landmarks, and currencies.

Pros:

  • Beautiful map-based interface
  • Decent variety of quiz modes (timed, endless, multiplayer)
  • Good visual design
  • Works offline

Cons:

  • Aggressive paywall. The free version locks most quiz categories and all customization options. Premium is $9.99/month or $49.99/year — steep for a geography quiz app.
  • No spaced repetition or smart review. It just cycles through random questions.
  • Multiplayer mode is dead. You'll rarely find opponents.

Verdict: If you're willing to pay $50/year, it's a good option. But there are better free alternatives.

2. Seterra — The Classic (Now with Baggage)

Seterra has been the gold standard for geography quizzes since the Flash era. It's comprehensive, with over 400 different quizzes covering every region, country, and topic imaginable. Teachers love it.

But in 2022, GeoGuessr (the popular location-guessing game) acquired Seterra. Since then, updates have slowed, and the app increasingly pushes users toward GeoGuessr's $30/year subscription.

Pros:

  • Massive quiz library (400+ quizzes)
  • Completely free with no ads (for now)
  • Great for targeted practice (e.g., "African capitals" or "U.S. state outlines")
  • Works offline

Cons:

  • The UI feels dated. It hasn't had a major redesign in years.
  • No gamification. No streaks, no progress tracking, no achievements. It's just quizzes.
  • No adaptive learning. Every quiz starts from scratch.
  • Increasingly buggy since the acquisition. Some users report crashes on newer iOS versions.

Verdict: Still a solid educational tool, especially for students and teachers. But the lack of updates and gamification makes it feel like abandonware. Use it while it lasts, but don't count on long-term support.

3. StudyGe — Ad-Supported Chaos

StudyGe tries to be the "free alternative" to World Quiz. It has a similar map-based interface and covers the basics — countries, capitals, flags. But the execution falls apart quickly.

Pros:

  • Actually free (no paywalls)
  • Decent question variety
  • Simple, straightforward quizzes

Cons:

  • Ads. Everywhere. Full-screen video ads after every quiz round. Sometimes two ads back-to-back. It kills momentum.
  • Requires internet for ads to load, even though the quiz data is local. If you're offline, it bugs out.
  • No progress tracking or gamification
  • Buggy. Frequent crashes and UI glitches.

Verdict: Only use this if you're desperate and broke. The ad experience is miserable.

4. Flag Play — Niche but Good at What It Does

Flag Play is laser-focused on one thing: learning flags. If that's your goal, it's the best option. If you want broader geography knowledge, look elsewhere.

Pros:

  • Best flag quiz app, period. High-quality images, comprehensive database.
  • Multiple modes: multiple choice, true/false, timed challenges
  • Clean, distraction-free UI
  • Works offline
  • Free with optional one-time $2.99 unlock for all features

Cons:

  • Only flags. No capitals, no borders, no landmarks. Extremely niche.
  • No spaced repetition
  • Limited gamification (just a basic score tracker)

Verdict: If you specifically need to memorize flags (e.g., for a competition or class), this is perfect. For general geography learning, it's too narrow.

  • Timed challenges with streak tracking. Compete against yourself or friends. The gamification actually works.
